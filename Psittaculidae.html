    • 604. Alexandrine Parakeet Psittacula eupatria (Linnaeus, 1766)
      Alternative Names: Large Indian Parakeet
      IUCN: Near Threatened. SoIB: Low. WLPA: Schedule-II. CITES: Appendix II.
      • 604a. Psittacula eupatria nipalensis (Hodgson, 1836) [HB#545]
        Original Description: Palaeornis Nipalensis Hodgson, 1836 Asiatic Researches; or, Transactions of the Society instituted in Bengal, for enquiring into the history, the antiquities, the arts and sciences, and literature of Asia 19 p.177
        Taxonomy: Includes indoburmanicus Hume 1878 from Sikkim.
        Distribution: Kashmir, Jammu, along the Himalayan foothills through Himachal, Uttarakhand, N Uttar Pradesh and Bihar, W Bengal, Sikkim and Brahmaputra plains of Assam and adjoining Arunachal and Nagaland, extending west through E Rajasthan, Gujarat, C India, and NE Ghats of Andhra and Odisha.
        Specimens: BNHS, ZSI, UMZC, UMMZ, AMNH, FMNH, MCZ, ROM, YPM
      • 604b. Psittacula eupatria eupatria (Linnaeus, 1766) [HB#546]
        Original Description: Psittacus Eupatria Linnaeus, 1766 Systema Naturæ. Editio duodecima reformata. Tomus I [part 1] 12, p.140 (Gingi, Tamil Nadu)
        Distribution: Mumbai region south through Goa, Karnataka along the outer edge of W Ghats as well as SE Ghats in the Deccan extending to Nallamala hills.
        Specimens: HYD
        Notes: 18°N kept as the northern limit for this population (AR2001). It has also been suggested that the populations of this species in western India may have been established by cage escapees (RA2012).
      • 604c. Psittacula eupatria magnirostris (Ball, 1872) [HB#548]
        Original Description: Palaeornis magnirostris Ball, 1872 Journal of the Asiatic Society of Bengal 41 p.278 (Andaman Is)
        Distribution: N, Middle, S, and Little Andaman Is., Narcondam and Barren Is., also islets of North Reef, Interview, Long, Havelock, Neil, Ross, Tarmugli, Alexandra, Red skin, Boat, South Sentinel, and Rutland Is.
        Specimens: BNHS, ZSI, ROM, USNM
      • 604d. Psittacula eupatria avensis (Kloss, 1917) [HB#547]
        Original Description: Palaeornis eupatria avensis Kloss, 1917 The Journal of the Natural History Society of Siam 2 no.3 p.219
        Distribution: Cachar.
        Specimens: BNHS
        Notes: Reports from Nagaland, Manipur, and Mizoram not evaluated.

    • 605. Rose-ringed Parakeet Psittacula krameri (Scopoli, 1769)
      Original Description: Psittacus krameri Scopoli, 1769 Annus I Historico-naturalis. Descriptiones avium musei proprii earumque rariorum, quas vidit in vivario Augustiss. Imperatorus, et museo excell. Comitis Francisci Annib. Turriani p.31
      IUCN: Least Concern. SoIB: Low. WLPA: Schedule-II.
      • 605a. Psittacula krameri borealis (Neumann, 1915) [HB#549]
        Original Description: Palaeornis krameri borealis Neumann, 1915 Ornithologische Monatsberichte 23 p.178 (Assam)
        Taxonomy: Includes fragosa Koelz 1954 from Anadra, Sirohi, Rajasthan.
        Distribution: Kashmir south through the subcontinent about the latitude of Mumbai; east into Assam, adjoining Arunachal as well as NE hill states.
        Type Specimen: ZMB#130849 (Holotype).
        Specimens: BNHS, ZSI, UMZC, UMMZ, AMNH, FMNH, MCZ, ROM, YPM
      • 605b. Psittacula krameri manillensis (Bechstein, 1800) [HB#550]
        Original Description: Psittacus Manillensis Bechstein, 1800 Naturgeschichte der Stubenthiere oder Anleitung zur Kentniss und Wartung derjenigen Thiere, welche man in der Stube halten kann: 1. Band. Die Stubenvögel. Zweite vermehrte und verbesserte Auflage Ed.2, 1 p.612, note
        Distribution: Throughout southern peninsula below the latitude of Mumbai; Lakshadweep Is.
        Specimens: BNHS, ZSI, UMZC, UMMZ, AMNH, FMNH, MCZ, ROM, USNM, YPM, TNHM, HYD
        Notes: 20°N kept as the northern limit for this population (AR2001).

    • 606. Slaty-headed Parakeet Psittacula himalayana (Lesson, 1832) [HB#562]
      Original Description: Psittacus (Conurus) Himalayanus Lesson, 1832 Voyage aux Indes Orientales par le Nord de l'Europe, les provinces du Caucase, la Géorgie, l'Arménie et la Perse (etc.). Zoologie pt4 p.239
      Taxonomy: Rothschild's or Intermediate Parakeet P. intermedia Rothschild 1895 (HB#561) now considered invalid and the specimens were of hybrid origin; specifically P. himalayana x P. cyanocephala and P. krameri x P. cyanocephala (Rasmussen & Collar 1999).
      Alternative Names: Himalayan Parakeet, Himalayan Slaty-headed Parakeet
      Distribution: W Himalaya from Kashmir all the way till Uttarakhand; and in Sikkim and N Bengal as well.
      Specimens: BNHS, NHMUK, UMMZ, AMNH, FMNH, USNM, YPM, LKO
      IUCN: Least Concern. SoIB: Low. WLPA: Schedule-II. CITES: Appendix II.

      • 607. Grey-headed Parakeet Psittacula finschii (Hume, 1874) [HB#563]
        Original Description: Palaeornis Finschii Hume, 1874 Stray Feathers 2 p.509
        Alternative Names: Finsch's Parakeet, Eastern Slaty-headed Parakeet
        Distribution: Historically from N Bengal east through Assam and NE hill states; presently restricted to E Arunachal, adjoining Assam, Nagaland, Cachar, Manipur, and Mizoram.
        Specimens: UMZC, UMMZ, AMNH, FMNH, YPM
        IUCN: Near Threatened. SoIB: Moderate. WLPA: Schedule-II. CITES: Appendix II.

        • 608. Plum-headed Parakeet Psittacula cyanocephala (Linnaeus, 1766) [HB#557-558]
          Original Description: Psittacus cyanocephalus Linnaeus, 1766 Systema Naturæ. Editio duodecima reformata. Tomus I [part 1] 12, p.141
          Taxonomy: N Indian bengalensis Foster 1781 (Type locality: Kolkata) sometimes recognised. Includes rosa Boddaert 1783 from Mahe – restricted to Malabar coast.
          Alternative Names: Blossom-headed Parakeet
          Distribution: Throughout Indian peninsula from Kashmir through the Himalayan ranges till Uttarakhand, N and NW India excluding the Thar, hills of W, and C India, east till Sikkim and W Bengal, hills of S India including W Ghats and the adjoining midlands, SE Ghats and Deccan hillocks. Sporadic in Deccan plateau and E coastal plains.
          Specimens: BNHS, ZSI, UMZC, UMMZ, AMNH, FMNH, MCZ, ROM, USNM, TNHM, HYD, LKO
          IUCN: Least Concern. SoIB: Low. WLPA: Schedule-II. CITES: Appendix II.

          • 609. Blossom-headed Parakeet Psittacula roseata Biswas, 1951
            Alternative Names: Eastern Blossom-headed Parakeet, Rosy-headed Parakeet
            IUCN: Near Threatened. SoIB: Moderate. WLPA: Schedule-II. CITES: Appendix II.
            • 609a. Psittacula roseata roseata Biswas, 1951 [HB#559]
              Original Description: Psittacula roseata Biswas, 1951 American Museum Novitates no.1500 p.4 (Gunjong, Assam)
              Taxonomy: Though Tripura birds trend towards juneae (Biswas 1951), true juneae do not occur within our limits.
              Distribution: N Bengal, Sikkim, through Assam, adjoining Arunachal, Nagaland, Meghalaya, Cachar, Manipur, and Tripura.
              Type Specimen: AMNH#621495 (Holotype).
              Specimens: BNHS, NHMUK, AMNH, FMNH, TNHM
              Notes: Mizoram records not evaluated.

          • 610. Malabar Parakeet Psittacula columboides (Vigors, 1830) [HB#564]
            Original Description: Palaeornis columboides Vigors, 1830 The Zoological Journal 5 p.274 (Anachady [=Aneichardi], Kollam district.)
            Alternative Names: Blue-winged Parakeet
            Distribution: W Ghats, including the midlands, from south of Tansa (NW Maharashtra) till the southern tip of the W Ghats in Kanyakumari district (Tamil Nadu). Also, SE Ghats reaching almost till Bengaluru as well as Severayan, Kolli, and Sirumalai hills and the eastern slopes of W Ghats in Karnataka.
            Specimens: BNHS, ZSI, UMZC, UMMZ, AMNH, FMNH, ROM, USNM, YPM, TNHM, LKO
            IUCN: Least Concern. SoIB: Low. WLPA: Schedule-II. CITES: Appendix II.
            Endemic: Western Ghats.

                • 615. Vernal Hanging-Parrot Loriculus vernalis (Sparrman, 1787)
                  Alternative Names: Indian Lorikeet
                  IUCN: Least Concern. SoIB: Low. WLPA: Schedule-II. CITES: Appendix II.
                  • 615a. Loriculus vernalis vernalis (Sparrman, 1787) [HB#566-567]
                    Original Description: Psittacus vernalis Sparrman, 1787 Museum Carlsonianum, in quo novas et selectas aves, coloribus ad vivum brevique descriptione illustratas (etc.) fasc.2 no.xxix,pl. (Cachar, Assam)
                    Taxonomy: Includes rubropygius Baker 1926 of W Ghats (Type locality Belagavi [=Belgaum] district). Andaman birds may represent an undescribed subspecies.
                    Distribution: Occurs as four disjunct populations. (1) Sikkim, N Bengal through Assam, adjoining Arunachal, Nagaland, Meghalaya, Cachar, Manipur, Mizoram, and Tripura. (2) NE Ghats of Odisha, NE Andhra, and Chhattisgarh extending to E Maharashtra. (3) W India from Mumbai south till Kanyakumari, extending along SE Ghats to Sirumalai, Servarayan, and Biligirirangan till Melagiri. (4) Andaman Is. – all major islands and islets. Sporadic elsewhere.
                    Specimens: BNHS, ZSI, AMNH, ROM, TNHM
                    Notes: Movements not completely worked out.
