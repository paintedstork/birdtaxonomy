    • 1043. Hodgson's Treecreeper Certhia hodgsoni - Brooks 1871
      Alternative Names: Eurasian Treecreeper (with C familiaris)
      IUCN: Least Concern. SoIB: Low. WLPA: Schedule-II.
      • [Certhia hodgsoni khamensis Bianchi 1903 [HB#1844]]
        Original Description: Certhia khamensis A hand-list of the genera and species of birds 4 p.355,360
        Distribution: Mechuka (N Arunachal).
        Notes: Ludlow & Kinnear (1944) does mention a specimen within the Indian side of the border; but the same could not be located.
      • 1043a. Certhia hodgsoni hodgsoni Brooks 1871 [HB#1842]
        Original Description: Certhia familiaris hodgsoni Proceedings of the Asiatic Society of Bengal 9 p.209 (Kashmir)
        Distribution: Gilgit, Astor, Kashmir, Jammu, and Lahaul-Spiti region of Himachal.
        Specimens: AMNH, FMNH
      • 1043b. Certhia hodgsoni mandellii Brooks 1874 [HB#1843]
        Original Description: Certhia Mandellii Journal of the Asiatic Society of Bengal 42 p.256 (Sikkim)
        Distribution: Himalaya from Kullu (Himachal), through Uttarakhand, N Bengal, Sikkim, and W Arunachal.
        Specimens: BNHS, UMMZ, FMNH, YPM
    • 1044. Bar-tailed Treecreeper Certhia himalayana - Vigors 1832
      Alternative Names: Himalayan Treecreeper
      IUCN: Least Concern. SoIB: Low. WLPA: Schedule-II.
      • 1044a. Certhia himalayana himalayana Vigors 1832 [HB#1845 & 1847-1848]
        Original Description: Certhia Himalayana Proceedings of the Committee of Science and Correspondence of the Zoological Society of London Part 1 no.14 p.174 (Kumaon or Garhwal, Uttarakhand)
        Taxonomy: Includes infima Ripley 1950 (E Uttarakhand and W Nepal) as well as limes Meinertzhagen 1922 (Gilgit and Kashmir).
        Distribution: Gilgit, Ladakh (straggler), Kashmir, Jammu, Himachal, and Uttarakhand – sporadic in adjacent Punjab, Haryana, and N Uttar Pradesh.
        Specimens: BNHS, ZSI, NHMUK, UMMZ, AMNH, FMNH, MCZ, USNM, YPM
        Notes: N Bengal report not investigated.
      • 1044b. Certhia himalayana yunnanensis Sharpe 1902
        Original Description: Certhia yunnanensis Bulletin of the British Ornithologists' Club 13 p.11
        Distribution: Walong region (NE Arunachal).
        Notes: All reports (including media) evidently of this subspecies based on range.
    • 1045. Rusty-flanked Treecreeper Certhia nipalensis - Blyth 1845 [HB#1851]
      Original Description: C.[erthia] nipalensis Journal of the Asiatic Society of Bengal 14 p.581
      Taxonomy: Includes stoliczkae Brooks 1874 from Sikkim.
      Alternative Names: Nepal Treecreeper
      Distribution: Uttarakhand, N Bengal, Sikkim, Arunachal till extreme east in Upper Noa-Dihing valley.
      Specimens: ZSI, NHMUK, AMNH, FMNH, MCZ, ROM, USNM, YPM
      IUCN: Least Concern. SoIB: Moderate. WLPA: Schedule-II.
      • 1046. Sikkim Treecreeper Certhia discolor - Blyth 1845 [HB#1849]
        Original Description: C.[erthia] discolor Journal of the Asiatic Society of Bengal 14 p.580 (Darjeeling)
        Alternative Names: Brown-throated Treecreeper
        Distribution: E Uttarakhand (local), N Bengal, Sikkim, W Arunachal – locally in C and NE Arunachal, Nagaland, and W Manipur (historical).
        Specimens: BNHS, ZSI, NHMUK, UMMZ, AMNH, FMNH, MCZ, ROM, YPM
        IUCN: Least Concern. SoIB: Moderate. WLPA: Schedule-II.

        • 1048. Indian Spotted Creeper Salpornis spilonota - (Franklin 1831)

          • 1048a. Salpornis spilonota rajputanae Meinertzhagen & Meinertzhagen 1926 [HB#1840]
            Original Description: Salpornis spilonotus rajputanae Bulletin of the British Ornithologists' Club 46 p.83 (Sambhar, Rajasthan)
            Distribution: Rajasthan.
            Specimens: BNHS, NHMUK
          • 1048b. Salpornis spilonota spilonota (Franklin 1831) [HB#1841]
            Original Description: Certhia spilonota Proceedings of the Committee of Science and Correspondence of the Zoological Society of London Part 1 no.10 p.121 (Between Kolkata and Varanasi)
            Distribution: SW Haryana, Delhi region (historical), Bharatpur (historical), Madhya Pradesh, N Gujarat, immediate adjacent parts of Rajasthan, N and E Maharashtra, N Telangana, NE Andhra, Chhattisgarh (historical), and Bihar/Jharkhand (historical) – local everywhere.
            Specimens: BNHS, NHMUK, AMNH, FMNH
            Notes: Declining taxon with range contraction.
