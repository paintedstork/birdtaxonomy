    • 625. White-bellied Minivet Pericrocotus erythropygius (Jerdon, 1840) [HB#1096]
      Original Description: M[uscicapa] erythropygia Jerdon, 1840 Madras Journal of Literature and Science 11 p.17 (Ajanta hills, Maharashtra)
      Distribution: Punjab (local?), Delhi region, E Rajasthan, Madhya Pradesh, Gujarat, Maharashtra, NE Karnataka, and Telangana – sporadically further east in Uttar Pradesh, Bihar, Jharkhand and Odisha. Disjunctly in the Deccan of S Karnataka, and NW Tamil Nadu at the base of the Nilgiris.
      Specimens: BNHS, NHMUK, UMMZ, AMNH, FMNH, HYD
      IUCN: Least Concern. SoIB: Moderate. WLPA: Schedule-II.
      Endemic: Endemic to India.

      • 626. Small Minivet Pericrocotus cinnamomeus (Linnaeus, 1766)
        IUCN: Least Concern. SoIB: Low. WLPA: Schedule-I.
        • 626a. Pericrocotus cinnamomeus peregrinus (Linnaeus, 1766) [HB#1091]
          Original Description: [Parus] peregrinus Linnaeus, 1766 Systema Naturæ. Editio duodecima reformata. Tomus I [part 1] 12, p.342 (Ambala, Haryana,)
          Taxonomy: Includes iredalei Baker 1929 from Ambala, Haryana and galbinus Van Tyne & Koelz 1936 from Kangra, Himachal.
          Distribution: Gangetic plains of Haryana, Uttarakhand, Uttar Pradesh, W Bengal, and NC India.
          Specimens: BNHS, NHMUK, UMMZ, AMNH, FMNH, MCZ, ROM, USNM, YPM, MYS, HYD
        • 626b. Pericrocotus cinnamomeus pallidus Baker, 1920 [HB#1090]
          Original Description: Pericrocotus peregrinus pallidus Baker, 1920 Bulletin of the British Ornithologists' Club 40 p.115
          Distribution: Sub-Himalayan tracts of Jammu, Himachal, Punjab, W Haryana, W Rajasthan, and Kachchh.
          Specimens: NHMUK, SMA
          Notes: Not always possible to assign subspecies – occurs in Punjab and Meerut, Uttar Pradesh while intervening specimens in Haryana are of peregrinus (see Abdulali 1981).
        • 626c. Pericrocotus cinnamomeus malabaricus (Gmelin, 1789) [HB#1094]
          Original Description: [Parus] malabaricus Gmelin, 1789 Systema Naturae. Editio decima tertia, aucta, reformata. Cura Jo. Frid. Gmelin (etc.). Tomus I. Pars II 13, vol. 1, part 2 1 pt2 p.1012 (Mahe)
          Distribution: SW India from about Goa south through coastal Karnataka and Kerala.
          Specimens: BNHS, NHMUK, UMMZ, AMNH, FMNH, MCZ, USNM, YPM
        • 626d. Pericrocotus cinnamomeus cinnamomeus (Linnaeus, 1766) [HB#1093]
          Original Description: [Motacilla] cinnamomea Linnaeus, 1766 Systema Naturæ. Editio duodecima reformata. Tomus I [part 1] 12, p.335
          Taxonomy: Includes sidhoutnsis Koelz 1939 from Sidhout, Kadapa, Andhra Pradesh and dharmakumari Koelz 1950 from Jamvala, Junagadh, Gujarat.
          Distribution: Saurashtra (Gujarat), S Rajasthan, Madhya Pradesh, S Odisha, Chhattisgarh and further south through the peninsula in Karnataka, Telangana, Andhra, and Tamil Nadu.
          Specimens: BNHS, ZSI, NHMUK, AMNH, FMNH, ROM, USNM
        • 626e. Pericrocotus cinnamomeus vividus Baker, 1920 [HB#1095]
          Original Description: Perecrocotus peregrinus vividus Baker, 1920 Bulletin of the British Ornithologists' Club 40 p.114
          Taxonomy: Sometimes, this subspecies is included under thai Deignan 1947 (see RA2012). Even if the populations are synonymised, thai should only be a junior synonym of vividus. Andaman birds included here, but may merit subspecies separation as osmastoni Roselaar & Prins 2000 (see Abdulali 1981).
          Distribution: E Bihar, W Bengal, Odisha, extreme NE Andhra, Sikkim, N Bengal, Assam, Nagaland, Cachar, Manipur, and Tripura. Disjunctly in N, Middle, S, and Little Andaman Is. as well as the islets of Interview, Long, Inglis, Havelock, Neil, Ross, Tarmugli, Boat, Red Skin, Jolly Buoy, N Cinque, and S Cinque Is.
          Specimens: BNHS, ZSI, NHMUK, UMMZ, FMNH, MCZ, ROM, USNM, YPM

      • 629. Long-tailed Minivet Pericrocotus ethologus Bangs & Phillips, 1914

          Type Specimen: MCZ#24146 (Holotype).

        • 629b. Pericrocotus ethologus laetus Mayr, 1940 [HB#1086]

          Distribution: N Bengal, Sikkim, Assam, Arunachal, and Meghalaya.
          Type Specimen: FMNH#84600 (Holotype).
          Specimens: BNHS, NHMUK, UMMZ, FMNH
        • [Pericrocotus ethologus ethologus Bangs & Phillips, 1914 ]
          Original Description: Pericrocotus brevirostris ethologus Bangs & Phillips, 1914 Bulletin of the Museum of Comparative Zoology 58 p.282
          Distribution: E Assam and E Arunachal.
          Specimens: NHMUK
          Notes: In the absence of validated specimens, presence of the nominate subspecies in NE India is hypothetical. Listing this for 'NE Assam' in NHMUK may be following older taxonomy (see RA2012).
        • 629c. Pericrocotus ethologus mariae Ripley, 1952 [HB#1087]
          Original Description: Pericrocotus ethologus mariae Ripley, 1952 Journal of the Bombay Natural History Society 50 p.486 (Phek, Nagaland)
          Taxonomy: Includes aureus Koelz 1952 of Mizo hills.
          Distribution: Nagaland, Cachar, Manipur, Mizoram, and Tripura.
          Type Specimen: YPM#12685 (Holotype).
          Specimens: NHMUK, UMMZ, YPM

      • 630. Orange Minivet Pericrocotus flammeus (Forster, 1781) [HB#1080]
        Original Description: Muscicapa flammea Forster, 1781 Indische Zoologie oder systematischer Beschreibungen seltener und unbekannter Thiere aus Indien (etc.) p.25 pl.15

              • 635. Large Cuckooshrike Coracina macei (Lesson, 1831)
                Alternative Names: [Javan Cuckooshrike]; Indian Cuckooshrike C m. macei & layardi
                IUCN: Least Concern. SoIB: Moderate. WLPA: Schedule-II.
                • 635a. Coracina macei macei (Lesson, 1831) [HB#1072]
                  Original Description: Graucalus Macei Lesson, 1831 Traité d'Ornithologie, ou Tableau Méthodique des ordres, sous-ordres, familles, tribus, genres, sous-genres et races d'oiseaux livr.5 p.349 (Kolkata)
                  Taxonomy: Sometimes considered a full species along with layardi Blyth 1866 of Sri Lanka (see RA2012).
                  Distribution: Throughout India from about Delhi region south through the Gangetic plains roughly south of Ganga river till W Bengal, E Rajasthan, Gujarat excluding Kachchh, C India, W Ghats, E Ghats, and associated hillocks, locally in Deccan.
                  Specimens: BNHS, ZSI, NHMUK, UMMZ, AMNH, FMNH, MCZ, ROM, USNM, YPM, MYS, HYD
                • 635b. Coracina macei nipalensis (Hodgson, 1836) [HB#1073]
                  Original Description: Graucalus nipalensis Hodgson, 1836 The India Review and Journal of Foreign Science and the Arts 1 p.327-328
                  Taxonomy: Includes lushaiensis Koelz 1954 from Sangau, Mizoram but subspecies assignment of Mizoram (and Manipur) birds tentative, as suggested in RA2012; affinity to extralimital siamensis Baker 1918 to be investigated.
                  Distribution: Along the Himalayan foothills from Jammu, through Himachal, Uttarakhand, terai region of N Uttar Pradesh and N Bihar, N Bengal, Sikkim, Assam, Arunachal, and all NE hill states.
                  Specimens: BNHS, ZSI, NHMUK, UMMZ, FMNH, ROM, USNM, YPM, LKO
                • 635c. Coracina macei andamana (Neumann, 1915) [HB#1075]
                  Original Description: Graucalus macei andamanus Neumann, 1915 Ornithologische Monatsberichte 23 p.181 (Andaman Is)
                  Taxonomy: Includes andamanensis Whistler 1940 from S Andamans.
                  Distribution: N, Middle, S, and Little Andaman Is as well as the islets of Interview, Long, Outram, Havelock, Neil, Tarmugli, Red Skin, and Rutland Is.
                  Specimens: BNHS, NHMUK, ROM, USNM

                • 637. Pied Triller Lalage nigra (Forster, 1781)
                  Original Description: Turdus niger Forster, 1781 Indische Zoologie oder systematischer Beschreibungen seltener und unbekannter Thiere aus Indien (etc.) p.41
                  Alternative Names: Pied Cuckooshrike
                  IUCN: Least Concern. WLPA: Schedule-II.
                  • 637a. Lalage nigra davisoni Kloss, 1926 [HB#1079a]
                    Original Description: Lalage nigra davisoni Kloss, 1926 Journal of the Malayan Branch of the Royal Asiatic Society 4 p.159 (Nicobar Is)
                    Taxonomy: Nomenclature complicated. Both historical names Campephaga terat and Turdus niger Foster 1781 for this population are invalid. Baker (1923) restricted the type locality to the nominate subspecies to Nicobars only to be over-ruled by Kloss (1926) who moved it to Singapore and gave a new name for the birds of Kamorta, Nicobars.
                    Distribution: C Nicobar Is. – Teresa, Trinket, Nancowry, Kamorta, and Katchal Is.
                    Specimens: BNHS
                    Notes: Sight report from Little Andaman Is. likely to be correct, but not evaluated here – old Andaman specimens were misidentified Ashy Minivet P. divaricatus (RA2012).

                • 638. Black-winged Cuckooshrike Lalage melaschistos (Hodgson, 1836)
                  Alternative Names: Dark Grey Cuckooshrike
                  IUCN: Least Concern. SoIB: Low. WLPA: Schedule-II.
                  • 638a. Lalage melaschistos melaschistos (Hodgson, 1836) [HB#1077]
                    Original Description: Volvocivora melaschistos Hodgson, 1836 The India Review and Journal of Foreign Science and the Arts 1 p.328
                    Taxonomy: Birds of E Arunachal and NE hill states show consistent plumage variations (RA2012); more study needed to see if a subspecies status is warranted.
                    Distribution: Breeding: Jammu (sporadic), Himachal, Uttarakhand, N Bengal, Sikkim, Arunachal, Assam, and all NE hill states. Non-breeding: Extends to adjacent plains and C Indian highlands, S W Bengal, Chota Nagpur plateau of Jharkhand, N Odisha, NE Ghats of Odisha, NE Andhra, and Chhattisgarh, sporadically further south and east reaching E Gujarat, Telangana, Goa, as far south as Bengaluru (SE Karnataka) and Chennai (NE Tamil Nadu).
                    Specimens: BNHS, ZSI, UMMZ, AMNH, FMNH, YPM, LKO

                • 639. Black-headed Cuckooshrike Lalage melanoptera (Rüppell, 1839)
                  IUCN: Least Concern. SoIB: Low. WLPA: Schedule-II.
                  • 639a. Lalage melanoptera melanoptera (Rüppell, 1839) [HB#1078]
                    Original Description: Ceblepyris melanoptera Rüppell, 1839 Museum Senckenbergianum: Abhandlungen aus dem Gebiete der beschreibenden Naturgeschichte 3 p.25 pl.2 fig.1 (Bhadwar, Himachal)
                    Taxonomy: Localised subspecies; Jammu birds assumed to belong to this subspecies. Includes eximia Van Tyne & Koelz 1936 from Bhadwar, Kangra, Himachal.

                    Type Specimen: SMF#139295 (Holotype).
                    Specimens: NHMUK, SMF, USNM
                    Notes: Exact southeastern limits of this subspecies unknown; tentatively assumed to intergrade with sykesi in Uttarakhand.
                  • 639b. Lalage melanoptera sykesi Strickland, 1844 [HB#1079]

                    Distribution: Throughout the subcontinent – from about Delhi region, E Rajasthan, Gujarat, Gangetic plains, C India, W Bengal (incl. N Bengal), Tripura, entire southern peninsula including Ghats, and Deccan.
                    Type Specimen: NHMUK#1880.1.1.2807 (Syntype).
                    Specimens: BNHS, ZSI, NHMUK, UMMZ, AMNH, FMNH, MCZ, YPM, TNHM, MYS, HYD
                    Notes: Presence in NE India (AR2001), except Tripura, required confirmation.
