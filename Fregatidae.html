    • 371. Lesser Frigatebird Fregata ariel (Gray, 1845)
      Alternative Names: Least Frigatebird
      IUCN: Least Concern. WLPA: Schedule-II.
      • [Fregata ariel ariel (Gray, 1845) ]
        Original Description: Atagen Ariel Gray, 1845 The genera of birds: comprising their generic characters, a notice of the habits of each genus, and an extensive list of species referred to their several genera pt.9, 3 p.669 pl.185
        Distribution: Andaman Sea.
        Notes: Andaman records may relate to this subspecies that breed southeast of the region.
      • 371a. Fregata ariel iredalei Mathews, 1914 [HB#32]
        Original Description: Fregata ariel iredalei Mathews, 1914 The Austral Avian Record; a scientific journal devoted primarily to the study of the Australian avifauna 2 no.6 p.121
        Distribution: Monsoon stragglers – mostly W coast, Lakshadweep, and rarely in E coast as well as inland.
        Specimens: BNHS

    • 372. Great Frigatebird Fregata minor (Gmelin, 1789)
      Original Description: Pelecanus minor Gmelin, 1789 Systema Naturae. Editio decima tertia, aucta, reformata. Cura Jo. Frid. Gmelin (etc.). Tomus I. Pars II 1 pt2 p.572
      Alternative Names: Lesser Frigatebird (vide AR2001)
      IUCN: Least Concern. WLPA: Schedule-II.
      • 372a. Fregata minor aldabrensis Mathews, 1914 [HB#31]
        Original Description: Fregata minor aldabrensis Mathews, 1914 The Austral Avian Record; a scientific journal devoted primarily to the study of the Australian avifauna 2 no.6 p.119
        Distribution: Monsoon stragglers – mostly in the SW coast, rarely in the E coast as well as inland.
        Specimens: BNHS, KAUNHM

    • 373. Christmas Island Frigatebird Fregata andrewsi Mathews, 1914 [HB#30]
      Original Description: Fregata andrewsi Mathews, 1914 The Austral Avian Record; a scientific journal devoted primarily to the study of the Australian avifauna 2 p.120
      Alternative Names: Christmas Frigatebird
      Distribution: Summer straggler. Reported from the coastal regions of West Bengal, Gujarat, Karnataka, Tamil Nadu, and Kerala.
      IUCN: Vulnerable. WLPA: Schedule-I. CITES: Appendix I. CMS: Appendix I.
