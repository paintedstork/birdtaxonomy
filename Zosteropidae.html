            • 930a. Zosterops palpebrosus occidentis Ticehurst 1927 [HB#1933]
              Original Description: Zosterops palpebrosa occidentis Bulletin of the British Ornithologists' Club 47 p.88 (Shimla)
              Taxonomy: Intergradation zone with the nominate subspecies not worked out but believed to be the terai region.
              Distribution: W Himalaya from Kashmir, sporadically in Ladakh, through Himachal, and Uttarakhand.
              Specimens: BNHS, ZSI, NHMUK, UMMZ, AMNH, FMNH, LKO
            • 930b. Zosterops palpebrosus palpebrosus (Temminck 1824) [HB#1933]
              Original Description: Sylvia palpebrosa Nouveau recueil de planches coloriées d'oiseaux, pour servir de suite et de complément aux planches enluminées de Buffon livr.49 pl.293 fig.3 sP (Bengal)
              Taxonomy: Lakshadweep birds sometimes (see IOC) suggested to be egregius Madarász 1911, which is otherwise endemic to Sri Lanka. Includes elwesi Baker 1922 from Sikkim, cacharensis Baker 1922 from Gunjong in N Cachar, and amabilis Koelz 1950 from Sasan, Junagadh, Kathiawar, Gujarat.
              Distribution: N India from Punjab, Haryana, through Delhi region, Gangetic plains, W Rajasthan, Gujarat, C India, Bihar, Jharkhand, W Bengal, Sikkim, Arunachal, Assam, all NE hill states, south through W Ghats and the transition zone, Deccan plateau, till about Kodagu, northern base of Nilgiris, Mysuru, and Bengaluru region. Also, in several atolls of Lakshadweep Is.
              Specimens: BNHS, ZSI, NHMUK, UMMZ, AMNH, FMNH, USNM, YPM, TNHM, LKO
            • 930c. Zosterops palpebrosus nilgiriensis Ticehurst 1927 [HB#1935]
              Original Description: Zosterops palpebrosa nilgiriensis Bulletin of the British Ornithologists' Club 47 p.89 (Coonoor, Tamil Nadu)
              Taxonomy: Includes palniensis Koelz 1939 (4 skins in BNHS) here, but probably requires subspecies status based on its larger size and substantial genetic differences (see Robin et al. 2010).
              Distribution: SW Ghats from Kodagu till Agasthyamala hills in S Tamil Nadu; also outcrops like Biligirirangan, Nilgiri, Palani, Varusanadu, Sirumalai, and Alagarkoil hills.
              Specimens: BNHS, NHMUK, AMNH, FMNH, USNM, YPM, TNHM
              Notes: Sirumalai and Alagarkoil birds included here rather than salimalii based on geographical proximity. Exact eastern limits of this subspecies not analysed.
            • 930d. Zosterops palpebrosus salimalii Whistler 1933 [HB#1934]
              Original Description: Zosterops palpebrosa salimalii Journal of the Bombay Natural History Society 36 p.811 (Farahabad, Telangana)
              Taxonomy: Intergradation points with the nominate subspecies on the west and south edge of its range unresolved.
              Distribution: CE Ghats from Nallamala hills, through Kadapa, Tirumala, to SE Ghats of Koundiya (S Andhra), Jawadhu, Yelagiri, Kalvarayan, Servarayan, and Kolli hills – roughly the E Ghats between the rivers of Godavari and Kaveri.
              Specimens: BNHS, ZSI, NHMUK, USNM, YPM, HYD
            • 930e. Zosterops palpebrosus nicobaricus Blyth 1845 [HB#1936]
              Original Description: Z[osterops] nicobaricus Journal of the Asiatic Society of Bengal Vol. 14, Part 2, N° 164 p.563 (Nicobar Is)
              Taxonomy: Andaman birds may constitute an undescribed subspecies. Taxonomic affinities of Nicobar birds with palpebrosus vis-a-vis other SE Asian White-eyes, specifically simplex Swinhoe 1861 and auriventer Hume 1878 unresolved.
              Distribution: N, Middle, S, and Little Andaman Is including islets of N Reef, Havelock, Neil, Tarmugli, Boat, and Rutland Is. All three groups of Nicobar – including Car Nicobar Is.; Tillanchong, Teresa, Bompoka, Kamorta, Nancowry, Trinket, and Katchal Is. all in the C Nicobar group; and Great Nicobar Is.
              Specimens: BNHS, NHMUK, ROM, USNM
