          • 594b. Falco tinnunculus interstinctus McClelland 1840 [HB#223]
            Original Description: Falco interstinctus Proceedings of the Zoological Society of London Pt7 no.82 p.154 (Assam)
            Distribution: Winter: N Bengal, Sikkim, Arunachal, Assam, and all NE hill states as well as Andaman & Nicobar Is. Sporadically in the Indian peninsula up to Coimbatore (Tamil Nadu).
            Specimens: BNHS, UMMZ, AMNH, FMNH, MCZ, YPM
            Notes: Breeding claims within Indian limits, particularly Sikkim and E Himalaya by Stuart Baker, and followed in Naoroji (2006), not considered definitive here. Andaman & Nicobar Is birds considered this subspecies purely on range.

          • 597. Amur Falcon Falco amurensis - Radde 1863 [HB#220]

            Distribution: Autumn passage migrant – concentrating in NE India as well as W Bengal and Sikkim; W India from Gujarat till Kanyakumari.               Specimens: BNHS, NHMUK, UMMZ, AMNH, MCZ, USNM, TNHM, LKO

                    Original Description: Falco pelegrinoides Nouveau recueil de planches coloriées d'oiseaux, pour servir de suite et de complément aux planches enluminées de Buffon livr.81 pl.479
                    Taxonomy: Wink (2000), Fuchs et al. (2015) found support for treating pelegrinoides as a separate species but also see Wink et al. (2010), White et al. (2013).

                  • 603b. Falco peregrinus babylonicus Sclater 1861 [HB#210]
                    Original Description: Falco babylonicus Ibis p.218 pl.7 (Oudh)
                    Taxonomy: When F. pelegrinoides is recognised as a species (RA 2012), babylonicus is listed as its subspecies.
                    Distribution: Winter: Kachchh, Rajasthan and sporadically elsewhere in peninsular India – from a region bounded by Kashmir and Ladakh in the north, Bankura (Bengal) in the east, and Belagavi (NW Karnataka) in the south.
                    Specimens: BNHS, ZSI, UMMZ, FMNH
                    Notes: See Bhatt & Ganpule (2017).
                  • 603c. Falco peregrinus peregrinator Sundevall 1837 [HB#211]
                    Original Description: Falco peregrinator Physiographiska Sällskapets Tidskrift 1 p.76
                    Taxonomy: RA2012 suggests that the northern and southern populations of this subspecies are disjunct and may be separated as two independent subspecies. More work required but certainly the Himalayan breeding population appear to be disjunct.
                    Distribution: W Himalaya from Kashmir, Ladakh, Himachal, and Uttarakhand; C Indian highlands and west till Mt. Abu, NE Ghats; W Ghats from Surat Dangs till S Tamil Nadu, SE Ghats as well as hillocks in the Deccan; sporadic in NE (e.g., Garo hills, W Arunachal).
                    Specimens: BNHS, ZSI, UMMZ, AMNH, FMNH, TNHM
