    • 474. Eastern Grass-Owl Tyto longimembris - (Jerdon 1839)
      Alternative Names: Australasian Grass Owl
      IUCN: Least Concern. WLPA: Schedule-II. CITES: Appendix II.
      • 474a. Tyto longimembris longimembris (Jerdon 1839) [HB#608]
        Original Description: Strix longimembris Madras Journal of Literature and Science 10 p.86 (Nilgiris)
        Distribution: E Assam, adjoining Arunachal, Nagaland, and Manipur valley. Historically, further W in Uttarakhand terai, N Bengal, Meghalaya, in hills of C and E peninsula as well as SW Ghats.
        Specimens: UMMZ, AMNH, FMNH, YPM
        Notes: Little known or possibly in decline due to degradation of grassland habitats.
    • 475. Common Barn-Owl Tyto alba - (Scopoli 1769)
      Original Description: Strix alba Annus I Historico-naturalis. Descriptiones avium musei proprii earumque rariorum, quas vidit in vivario Augustiss. Imperatorus, et museo excell. Comitis Francisci Annib. Turriani p.21
      Alternative Names: Eastern Barn Owl T. alba javanica
      IUCN: Least Concern. WLPA: Schedule-I. CITES: Appendix II.
      • 475a. Tyto alba stertens Hartert 1929 [HB#606]
        Original Description: Tyto alba stertens Novitates Zoologicae 35 p.98 (Cachar, Assam)
        Taxonomy: A single global species T. alba (Scopoli, 1769) no longer tenable and has been split into multiple species of which the 'Eastern' group T. [alba] javanica (Gmelin, 1788) occurs in India. However, some taxonomies (e.g., eBird/Clements) still follow a single global species approach, and hence followed here as well.
        Distribution: Throughout India except the high and trans Himalaya and Thar desert. Present in Brahmaputra plains of Assam and adjoining Arunachal but local elsewhere in NE India. Lakshadweep Is (introduced).
        Specimens: BNHS, ZSI, NHMUK, AMNH, FMNH, TNHM, LKO
    • 476. Andaman Barn-Owl Tyto deroepstorffi - (Hume 1875) [HB#607]
      Original Description: Strix De-Roepstorffi Stray Feathers 3 p.390 (Aberdeen, South Andaman Is.)
      Taxonomy: Sometimes treated as a subspecies of T. alba (see HBW/BLI).
      Alternative Names: Andaman Masked Owl
      Distribution: N, Middle, S and Little Andaman Is. and the islets of East, Thornhill, Paget, Interview, Viper, Ross, and Cinque Is.
      Specimens: BNHS, NHMUK
      IUCN: Not Recognised. WLPA: Schedule-I. CITES: Appendix II.
      Endemic: Andaman & Nicobar Is.
