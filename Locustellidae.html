      Original Description: Motacilla Certhiola Zoographia Rosso-Asiatica, sistens omnium animalium in extenso Imperio Rossico (etc.) 1 p.509

      IUCN: Least Concern. SoIB: Moderate. WLPA: Schedule-II. CMS: Appendix II.
      • 783a. Helopsaltes certhiola rubescens (Blyth 1845) [HB#1543]
        Original Description: Locustella rubescens Journal of the Asiatic Society of Bengal Vol. 14, Part 2, N° 164 p.582 (Neighbourhood of Kolkata)
        Taxonomy: ZSI#18813 from Kolkata is also listed as a 'type'.
        Distribution: Winter: W Bengal, Assam, Manipur valley, Andaman & Nicobar Is. Also, in southern peninsular India mostly along the SW coast but sporadically in Deccan and in SE coast.
        Specimens: ZSI, NHMUK, UMMZ
      • [Helopsaltes certhiola sparsimstriatus (Meise 1934) ]
        Original Description: Locustella certhiola sparsimstriata Abhandlungen und Berichte des Königlichen Zoologischen und Anthropologisch-Ethnographischen Museums zu Dresden 18 (2) p.39
        Distribution: Winter: S India.
        Notes: May occur (RA2012) but winter range not established (Kennerley & Pearson 2010). On range, some birds wintering in S India could be from this population.
      • [Helopsaltes certhiola centralasiae (Sushkin 1925) [HB#1542]]
        Original Description: Locustella certhiola centralasiae Proceedings of the Boston Society of Natural History 38 p.46
        Distribution: Winter: Andaman & Nicobar Is.
        Notes: AR2001 lists Andaman & Nicobar Is specimens as this subspecies. RA2012 did not definitely include this subspecies for our region. Abbott's Kamorta specimen (USNM 178748) listed as minor David & Oustalet 1877, was probably centralasiae.

            • 793. Broad-tailed Grassbird Schoenicola platyurus - (Jerdon 1841) [HB#1546]
              Original Description: Thimalia platyura Madras Journal of Literature and Science 13(1) p.170 (Gudalur, foot of the Nilgiris)
              Taxonomy: Plumage differences between northern and southern populations of the species (RA2012) probably related to individual, sexual or seasonal variations (Sant et al. 2019).
              Alternative Names: Broad-tailed Grass Warbler
              Distribution: W Ghats and adjoining transition zone of the Deccan. Locally in NW Ghats of Nashik, Pune region, Belagavi but more contiguous from SW Karnataka till southern tip of SW Ghats in Kanyakumari district (Tamil Nadu). Isolated records from NE Ghats of Andhra, Chengalpattu and Point Calimere (both Tamil Nadu) indicate some movements.
              Specimens: BNHS, NHMUK, YPM, TNHM
              IUCN: Vulnerable. SoIB: High. WLPA: Schedule-I.
              Endemic: Western Ghats.
              • 794. Bristled Grassbird Schoenicola striatus - (Jerdon 1841) [HB#1547]
                Original Description: Megalurus? striatus Catalogue of the birds of the Peninsula of India arranged according to the modern system of classification; with brief notes on their habits and geographical distribution, and descriptions of new, doubtful and imperfectly described species (Supplement) p.88 (Nilgiris)
                Alternative Names: Bristled Grass Warbler
                Distribution: Breeding: Locally along the Indo-Gangetic and Brahmaputra plains as well as E Rajasthan, Gujarat and S W Bengal. Non-breeding: Widely, but very locally in the Indian subcontinent till the southern states of Kerala and Tamil Nadu.
                Specimens: BNHS, NHMUK, UMMZ, AMNH, FMNH
                IUCN: Vulnerable. SoIB: High. WLPA: Schedule-I. CMS: Appendix II.
