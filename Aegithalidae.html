      Alternative Names: Stoliczka's Tit Warbler
      IUCN: Least Concern. SoIB: Moderate. WLPA: Schedule-II. CMS: Appendix II.
      • 893a. Leptopoecile sophiae sophiae Severtsov, 1873 [HB#1633]
        Original Description: Leptopoecile Sophiae Severtsov, 1873 Izvestiya Imperatorskago Obshchestva Lyubitelei Estestvoznaniya, Antropologii i Etnografii 8 pt2 p.66,135 pl.8 fig.8,9
        Distribution: Gilgit and Ladakh.
        Specimens: UMMZ, FMNH, ROM
      • 893b. Leptopoecile sophiae obscurus Przevalski, 1887 [HB#1634]
        Original Description: Leptopoecile obscura Przevalski, 1887 Zapiski Imperatorskoi Akademii Nauk Vol. 55, N° 1 p.80
        Distribution: W Arunachal (Sela and Tawang region).
        Notes: Photographs from Arunachal almost certainly this subspecies (see Praveen et al. 2019). Reports from Darjeeling not verified.

    • 894. Crested Tit-Warbler Leptopoecile elegans Przevalski, 1887 [HB#1632]
      Original Description: Leptopoecile elegans Przevalski, 1887 Zapiski Imperatorskoi Akademii Nauk 55 p.77
      Distribution: Vagrant. Reported only from north of Tawang (Arunachal).
      IUCN: Least Concern. WLPA: Schedule-II. CMS: Appendix II.

    • Aegithalos Hermann, 1804 Observationes zoologicae quibus novae complures, aliaeque animalium species describuntur et illustrantur p.214 Pipra europaea Hermann, 1804; Type by original designation = Parus caudatus Linnaeus, 1758
      • 895. White-cheeked Tit Aegithalos leucogenys (Moore, 1854) [HB#1821]
        Original Description: Orites leucogenys Moore, 1854 A catalogue of the birds in The Museum of the Hononrable East-India Company 1 p.374
        Alternative Names: White-cheeked Bushtit
        Distribution: Gilgit.
        Specimens: AMNH
        IUCN: Least Concern. WLPA: Schedule-II.
        Endemic: Western Himalaya.

        • 896. Black-throated Tit Aegithalos concinnus (Gould, 1855)
          Original Description: Psaltria concinna Gould, 1855 The birds of Asia 2 pt7 pl.65
          Alternative Names: Black-throated Bushtit; [Red-headed Tit]
          IUCN: Least Concern. SoIB: Low. WLPA: Schedule-II. CMS: Appendix II.
          • 896a. Aegithalos concinnus manipurensis (Hume, 1888) [HB#1820]
            Original Description: Aegithaliscus manipurensis Hume, 1888 Stray Feathers Vol. 11, N° 1-4 p.254 (in text) (E Manipur hills)
            Distribution: SE Arunachal (Upper Noa-Dihing valley), Nagaland, Meghalaya, Manipur, and Mizoram.
            Type Specimen: NHMUK#1886.11.1.622 (Syntype).
            Specimens: ZSI, NHMUK, UMMZ, FMNH, USNM, YPM
            Notes: Its presence in Cachar needs validation, but likely.
          • 896b. Aegithalos concinnus iredalei (Baker, 1920) [HB#1818]
            Original Description: Aegithaliscus concinna iredalei Baker, 1920 Bulletin of the British Ornithologists' Club 41 p.9 (Shimla)
            Taxonomy: New name for erythrocephalus Vigors 1831.
            Distribution: Kashmir, Jammu, Himachal, and Uttarakhand.
            Specimens: BNHS, ZSI, NHMUK, UMMZ, AMNH, FMNH, MCZ, ROM, USNM, YPM
          • 896c. Aegithalos concinnus rubricapillus (Ticehurst, 1925) [HB#1819]
            Original Description: Aegithaliscus concinna rubricapillus Ticehurst, 1925 Bulletin of the British Ornithologists' Club 46 p.22 (Sikkim)
            Taxonomy: Not always recognised (see RA2012).
            Distribution: N Bengal, Sikkim, Arunachal – till Lohit valley.
            Type Specimen: NHMUK#1898.9.20.364 (Holotype).
            Specimens: BNHS, ZSI, NHMUK

        • 897. White-throated Tit Aegithalos niveogularis (Gould, 1855) [HB#1822]
          Original Description: Acanthiparus niveogularis Gould, 1855 The birds of Asia 2 pt7 pl.67 (Himachal)
          Alternative Names: White-throated Bushtit
          Distribution: W Himalaya from Kashmir, Jammu, Himachal, and Uttarakhand.
          Type Specimen: NHMUK#1858.12.2.37 (Syntype).
          Specimens: NHMUK, UMMZ, AMNH, FMNH, MCZ
          IUCN: Least Concern. SoIB: Moderate. WLPA: Schedule-II.
          Endemic: Western Himalaya.

          • 898. Black-browed Tit Aegithalos iouschistos (Blyth, 1845)
            Alternative Names: Black-browed Bushtit; [Rufous-fronted Tit, Rufous-fronted Bushtit]
            IUCN: Least Concern. WLPA: Schedule-II. CMS: Appendix II.
            • 898a. Aegithalos iouschistos iouschistos (Blyth, 1845) [HB#1823]
              Original Description: Parus iouschistos Blyth, 1845 Journal of the Asiatic Society of Bengal p.943
              Distribution: N Bengal, Sikkim, W Arunachal, and very rarely in E Arunachal (Mishmi hills).
              Specimens: BNHS, ZSI, NHMUK, MCZ
              Notes: Overlap with bonvaloti Oustalet 1892 nearly nil within India though the nominate subspecies occurs in Tibet along the Tsangpo bend. Mishmi hill birds are probably stragglers from that population, and not a breeding one.
            • 898b. Aegithalos iouschistos bonvaloti (Oustalet, 1892)
              Original Description: Acredula Bonvaloti Oustalet, 1892 Annales des Sciences Naturelles Zoologie (7), 12 p.286
              Distribution: Walong region only.
              Notes: See Sangha et al. (2013).
